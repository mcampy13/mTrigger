What is the mTrigger?

mTrigger™ personal biofeedback device gamifies the physical therapy experience, increasing engagement and compliance during both in-clinic and at-home physical therapy.

mTrigger uses surface electromyography (sEMG) instrumentation to measure the electrical activity produced during muscle contractions. Other commonly used therapy devices such as electronic stimulators (e-stim) apply electrical stimulation to muscles without patient engagement; mTrigger, however, measures the electrical activity produced by muscles when a patient controls muscle activity neurologically, rehabilitating muscles via neuromuscular re-education. The device uses this data to control interactive biofeedback games and activities, allowing patients to play, train, and track their performance over the course of therapy.

Most biofeedback devices on the market are cumbersome, clinically focused, and expensive - but mTrigger is different. Fully portable and wearable, emotionally engaging, and significantly less expensive other sEMG biofeedback devices, mTrigger is a fun, smart way to get PT patients engaged in their therapy programs.

Setup is simple: apply electrodes, connect via Bluetooth, and calibrate. During calibration, the device records the patient’s maximum voluntary contraction (MVC), the standard metric for analyzing muscle intensity. Sessions are evaluated on the basis of MVC, and results are presented in a clear, comprehensible interface.

How is mTrigger Used In Clinics?

By facilitating engagement, mTrigger enables patients to understand their healthcare experience and connect more comprehensively with a key health care provider. The patient can see his or her performance in real time and get feedback from the physical therapist. Patients are motivated to get the most out of their therapy exercises, and therapists have an objective understanding of patient performance and progress.

How is mTrigger Used At Home?

mTrigger helps to motivate and track patient compliance outside the clinic. It can be used anywhere the patient may be, as its Bluetooth capability allows for a completely autonomous therapy system.

About mTrigger

Our Mission:

To make fun and innovation a part of physical therapy, and encourage patient engagement in the rehabilitation experience. We utilize mobile health to deliver innovative, user-friendly devices and mobile applications to supplement physical therapy rehab and improve health outcomes.

History of mTrigger:

mTrigger began as a simple idea: improve the physical therapy experience for patients and therapists. After thirty years in the field, Russ Paine knew that it was the right time to introduce a better way for patients to engage in their rehabilitation. Paine teamed up with laser therapy specialist and serial entrepreneur, Brian Pryor, to get the ball rolling. But how to get to market?

Cue: University of Delaware’s Office of Economic Innovation & Partnerships (OEIP) Spin In Program. With both founders being proponents of hands-on education, this opportunity to work with an interdisciplinary team of undergraduates was a perfect fit. The project began in the fall of 2014 and has accumulated diverse members and experience at every turn. Prototyping of the mTrigger continued through the spring and summer of 2015, with [the development team] making technological advances and learning to embrace the entrepreneurial spirit along the way.

Brian Pryor, PH.D.

Brian Pryor is the co-founder and CEO at LiteCure, LLC in Newark, DE. He has developed and taken to market several lasers and light based technologies.

Brian is well published, having contributed to 35 papers and several book chapters in the areas of chemistry, physics, laser development and applications, including lasers in medicine. Russ is well known for his experience in sports medicine and has special interests that include injuries to the knee and shoulder, as well as golfing injuries and conditioning.
